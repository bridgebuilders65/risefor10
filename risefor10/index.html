Somewhere back at the beginning, before the hours and the metrics and the paperwork, there was a reason you chose this work. Most days are too full to remember it. This is a way back to it.
RiseFor10
Give back ten percent of yourself — not ten percent of your schedule, but ten percent of your attention, your patience, your willingness to go a little further than you had to. It doesn't need a grand gesture. It just needs to be on purpose.

What counts
A second opinion given off the clock, because someone needed to hear it from a doctor
Sitting with a frightened family member a little longer than the chart requires
Helping a patient understand their diagnosis, not just receive it
Encouraging someone healthy to register as a blood or marrow donor
A free consultation, surgery, or procedure for someone who couldn't otherwise afford it
Reminding a colleague why they chose this work in the first place
No deadline. No quota. Just notice the moment and take it.

✦ Where This Began
RiseFor65
In 2026, on Didi Krishna's 65th birthday, doctors across 14 countries answered a simple call: serve one patient, in whatever way you could. Sixty-three physicians did. Some performed surgeries. Some waived a fee. Some sat with a patient who had no one else.

✦ Where The Inspiration Comes From
The Sadhu Vaswani Mission
For over 94 years, the Sadhu Vaswani Mission has lived by one idea: every act of service is also an act of love. Today it runs hospitals, schools, and relief programs in over 60 centers worldwide — work rooted in the same belief that brought RiseFor65 and RiseFor10 into being.
